Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before your first visit — and a few things regulars ask too.

Membership

Do I need a membership to visit?

Yes. Cube & Triangle is a members-only club. You'll need to apply for membership before your first visit. It takes a couple of minutes online and costs from £6/year.

How do I join?

Apply online first, then come to the venue at 40 Silver Street with valid photo ID showing your date of birth. Pay your membership at the bar. You're in.

What ID do I need?

Valid photo ID showing your date of birth — a passport, driving licence, or PASS card. Without ID, membership is limited to one month. Discounted rates (Senior, Student, Junior, Public Servant) also require proof of eligibility.

Can I pay membership online?

You must pay in the venue on your first visit. Bring your ID and pay at the bar.

How much does membership cost?

From £6/year for a standard annual membership. Reduced rates are available for Seniors, Students, Juniors and Public Servants. A one-month option is also available.

Can I renew membership online?

Renewals are handled at the venue. Come to the bar with your ID when your membership is due.

Guests & Visitors

Can I bring a guest?

Yes, but you must have been a paid-up member for at least 24 hours first. Membership can only be paid in the venue. A guest day fee of £7 per person per visit applies.

How many guests can I bring?

Check with staff at the bar — the number may depend on availability and current club rules. Each guest pays the £7 day fee.

Can non-members watch?

Non-members must pay the guest day fee of £7 to enter the venue. This applies to spectators as well as players.

Are juniors (under 18s) allowed?

Junior memberships are available. Please contact the club directly for details on junior access and supervision requirements. Call 01522 527939.

Booking & Tables

How do I book a table?

Once you're a paid-up member, book online here. You can also walk in, but snooker tables in particular book up — we recommend reserving ahead.

Can I walk in without a booking?

Yes. Pool and darts are often available on the day. Snooker can be busy, especially evenings and weekends — booking ahead is strongly recommended.

How are tables priced?

Rates are per table per hour — for your whole group, not per person. Pool is £6–£9/hour, Snooker £7–£10/hour, Darts £5–£7/hour depending on the time of day. See full pricing →

Where are the tables?

The club is over two floors. Ground floor has American pool and darts. Upstairs has English pool, snooker, and one American pool table. View the table plan →

Do you provide cues and equipment?

Yes — all cues, rests and equipment are provided. You're welcome to bring your own cue if you prefer.

Visiting the Club

Where are you located?

40 Silver Street, Lincoln, LN2 1EH — right in the city centre, a short walk from Lincoln Cathedral and the Bailgate area.

What are your opening hours?

Monday–Wednesday: 10am–11pm. Thursday–Saturday: 10am–Midnight. Sunday: 10am–11pm. Hours may vary on bank holidays — check our Facebook page for updates.

Is there parking nearby?

Several council car parks are within walking distance in central Lincoln, including Beaumont Fee and Tentercroft Street. Street parking is available nearby in the evenings.

Is the venue accessible?

Ground floor facilities are step-free. Please call us ahead of your visit if you have specific accessibility requirements and we'll do our best to help.

Do you serve food and drink?

Yes. Order directly to your table via the app — draft beers, spirits, soft drinks, milkshakes, snacks and hot food. We hold a 5-star food hygiene rating. See the full menu →

Do you accept card payments?

Yes — we accept all major debit and credit cards as well as contactless payments.

Is there live sport showing?

Yes. We show live sport on screens throughout the venue. See what's on →
